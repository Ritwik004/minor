Teachers Day celebration

"Teachers are the torch bearers of the Gen-next” Teachers play a major role in making their students responsible citizens of tomorrow and good human beings. They are the cornerstone of our future. We can never thank teachers enough for their immense contribution in a student’s life. Teacher's Day is celebrated to show our acknowledgement and recognition of the hard work put in by our teachers towards our development. Teachers Day at Gyan Ganga Institute of Technology and Sciences was celebrated with full zeal and enthusiasm. The day started with giving a tribute to Dr. Saravpalli Radhakrishnan. The day had an emotional and recreational touch. The students did all the preparations with complete energy and excitement. There were various games for the teachers and the students also performed various dances.

Lakshya 2k17 :

Lakshya 2k16 : Gyan Ganga's Literary Festival, affectionately known as Lakshya 2k16, celebrated the oral and written word. Each of the competitions held throughout the course of fest highlighted literature beyond what is taught in the classroom. From story writing to poetry slams, the purpose of this festival is not only to provide entertainment, but to celebrate and emphasize the importance of writing beyond what is required in school. Students showed a massive participation and were encouraged to share their own work.

Entrepreneurship Awareness Camp:

Entrepreneurship Awareness Camps (EACs) is conducted by Gyan Ganga Institute of Technology and Sciences. Each Camp, of 3 days duration, is conducted in the premises of GGITS and aims at creating awareness among students about various facets of entrepreneurship while highlighting the merits of pursuing such a career option. In each EAC, about 75-100 students are exposed to different aspects of entrepreneurship, including opportunity guidance, services offered by agencies of support system etc. A visit to the industries located in the region is also arranged to bring the students in direct touch with practicing entrepreneurs.

Srijan 2017

Srijan, brain child of Sister Nivedita Takniki Siksha Samiti, is a festival for engineering and pharmacy students organised at various engineering and pharmacy institutes of Madhya Pradesh. It aims to provide a platform to the students to showcase their creativity and innovativeness either by way of making models or by presenting technical research papers in their respective branches. It also provides students with an opportunity to participate in many other activities like Quiz, Rangoli, Robotics, Poster Etc. Srijan takes pride in bringing about a change in the mindset of some of the engineering colleges of the region. Due to overcapacity in engineering education, most colleges had resorted to publishing advertisements, giving discounts and using services of brokers.
